What Do We Have to Know About Online Slots

Slots constitute almost three-quarters of online casino games and have moved on from the days of the one-armed bandit. Their popularity is largely due to the player not needing any complex skills in relation to strategy since slots are based entirely on luck, with the possibility of winning large amounts from limited stakes. Slots offer an immersive experience from sports, fantasy and other entertainment that means there is always something new to try. Here are some things you need to know about online slots to make the best of your experience:

Pay lines

Paylines are the series of winning combinations that criss-cross the slots reels. They are the jagged lines that are drawn across the reels of a slot and play a large role in how winning combinations are formed. Single playlines are found in classic slots with three reels and three rows, with the payline consisting of just three matching symbols. However, there can be many more, depending on the number of reels and rows. For example, a modern 5-reel slot with four rows can have as many as 25, 30 or 50 paylines with a winning payline containing only two matching symbols, or two matching symbols plus Wilds or Scatters.

Most slots let you choose how many paylines you bet on each spin. Usually, you work with three buttons. You can select any or all paylines manually just by clicking on the numbers running up the side of the reels. You might select 2, 5, or 12 paylines to play with the bet size growing with each selection. Paylines run from Reel 1 on the left, which connects to a symbol on Reel 2, Reel 3, and so on. Payouts are usually made by connecting at least two symbols, but all must connect with Reel 1 in some way. Check out the paytable for each slot, which will provide further information about symbols, jackpots and bonus features, listing every payline pattern in the game, and the game's Return to Player (RTP) percentage.
